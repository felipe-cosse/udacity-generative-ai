# Summary: Generative AI Overview - What is Generative AI?

## Core Definition
Generative AI is a specialized subset of Artificial Intelligence (AI) focused on creating entirely new, realistic content (text, images, audio, video, or code) by learning patterns and structures from existing data. 

Unlike traditional AI, which primarily analyzes data, predicts outcomes, or classifies information, Generative AI shifts the paradigm toward creation and innovation.

## The AI Hierarchy
Generative AI fits into the broader ecosystem as follows:
1. **Artificial Intelligence (AI):** The overarching concept of machines mimicking human problem-solving and decision-making.
2. **Machine Learning (ML):** A subset of AI enabling computers to improve at tasks without explicit programming.
3. **Deep Learning:** A subset of ML utilizing artificial neural networks inspired by the human brain.
4. **Generative AI:** The newest subset of deep learning, typically guided by natural language to generate novel outputs.

## Technological Enablers: Foundation Models
* **Shift in Engineering:** Instead of training models from scratch, engineers leverage **Foundation Models**—massive systems trained on vast amounts of data—and adapt them for downstream tasks.
* **Training Method:** These models use **self-supervision**, where the model predicts omitted parts of its own input data (often scraped from the web). This bypassed the traditional "data labeling bottleneck."
* **Scaling:** Scaled foundation models evolved into **Large Language Models (LLMs)** and **Vision Language Models (VLMs)**. Mechanistically, an LLM acts as a highly scaled, statistical "next-probable-word" predictor.
* **Primary Use Cases:** Document summarization, question answering, and targeted data extraction.
