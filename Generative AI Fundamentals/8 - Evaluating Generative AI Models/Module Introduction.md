Evaluating generative AI models is a critical yet challenging aspect of AI engineering. Unlike traditional machine learning, generative AI produces probabilistic and open ended outputs, making it difficult to assess against predefined ground truths. While human evaluation remains a gold standard, its time, its high cost, make using AI itself as an evaluator an increasingly enticing alternative with its own set of challenges. Effective evaluation is crucial for successfully transitioning AI applications from pilot projects to production, guiding their development, and ensuring they consistently deliver high quality.
