# Summary: Benchmarking GenAI Models

## Core Concept
Benchmarking is the process of measuring and comparing the performance of AI models over time across different organizations on specific tasks. Unlike general evaluation, its main objective is to establish a standardized playing field to drive competition, innovation, and verifiable scientific progress.

## Historical Context: The ImageNet Moment
* **Pre-2009:** Computer vision research was limited by small, fragmented datasets, making it difficult to test model generalizability.
* **2009:** ImageNet introduced over 1 million labeled images across 1,000 categories.
* **2012:** AlexNet (a deep neural network) vastly outperformed traditional models on ImageNet, proving the potential of deep learning and accelerating modern AI research.

## Key Benefits of Benchmarks
* **Comparability:** Allows direct algorithmic comparisons to identify the best approaches.
* **Reproducibility:** Offers a shared foundation to verify scientific results.
* **Focus:** Targets community innovation toward well-defined problems.
* **Democratization:** Levels the playing field by providing open-access data to global researchers.
* **Acceleration:** Forces benchmarks to evolve to complex tiers as models surpass human performance.

## Key GenAI Benchmarks
* **SWE-bench:** Software engineering tasks using real GitHub issue-to-PR pairs. Models must modify code bases and pass tests.
* **GPQA (Graduate-Level Google-Proof Q&A):** 198 difficult multiple-choice science questions (biology, physics, chemistry) written by PhD experts, designed to resist simple search engine queries.
* **AIME 2024:** Math problems sourced from the American Invitational Mathematics Examination containing 30 integer-answer questions.
* **Custom Benchmarks:** Highly tailored internal benchmarks created by organizations to address specific business use cases.
