Artificial intelligence is undergoing a transformative shift driven by the rise of foundation models, which are large-scale neural networks possessing unprecedented performance across diverse tasks. These models are capable of versatile adaptation. Moving beyond traditional AI approaches that rely on specialized algorithms, these powerful models offer remarkable generalization and scalability, reshaping our understanding of machine intelligence and its potential. Exploring their architectures, trading processes, and real-world adaptation is essential to understanding how foundation models will shape the future trajectory of AI research and application.
